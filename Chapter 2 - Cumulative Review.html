Chapter 2 - Cumulative Review¶

1. Find the domain of \(h(x) = \dfrac{x-4}{x^2 + 5x - 36}\)¶

Solution:

\( x^2 + 5x - 36 \\ = x^2 + 9x - 4x - 36 \\ = x(x+9) -4(x+9) \\ = (x+9)(x-4) \)

\( h(x) = \dfrac{(x-4)}{(x+9)(x-4)} = \dfrac{1}{x+9} \)

\(h(x)\) can be defined when \(x+9 \ne 0\).

Therefore, the domain of the function: \( x < -9 \\ x > -9 \)

2. Find the domain of \(g(x) = \dfrac{x+2}{|x+2|}\)¶

Solution:

For \(g(x)\) to be defined, \(|x+2| \ne 0 \)

Therefore, the domain of \(g(x)\): \( x<-2 \\ x>-2 \)

\( \begin{align} g(x) &= 1 \ &\forall x > -2 \\ &= -1 \ &\forall x < -2 \end{align} \)

4. Solve: \(\sqrt{2x+3} = 2\)¶

Solution:

\( \sqrt{2x+3} = 2 \\ (\sqrt{2x+3})^2 = 2^2 \\ \implies 2x + 3 = 4 \\ \implies x = \dfrac{1}{2} \)

5. Solve: \(|2x+3| = 4\)¶

Solution:

\( |2x+3| = 4 \\ 2x + 3 = 4 \qquad {(1)} \\ x = \dfrac{1}{2} \\ or, \\ 2x + 3 = -4 \qquad {(2)} \\ x = -\dfrac{7}{2} \\ x = (\dfrac{1}{2}, -\dfrac{7}{2}) \)

6. Solve: \(|2x-5| \lt 3\)¶

Solution: \( |2x-5| \lt 3 \\ 2x -5 \lt 3 \qquad {(1)} \\ x \lt 4 \\ or, \\ 2x -5 \gt -3 \qquad {(2)} \\ x \gt 1 \\ 1 < x < 4 \)

7. Solve: \(|4x + 1| \ge 7\)¶

Solution:

\( |4x + 1| \ge 7 \\ (4x + 1) \ge 7 \qquad {(1)} \\ x \ge \dfrac{3}{2} \\ (4x + 1) \le -7 \qquad {(2)} \\ x \le -2 \)
